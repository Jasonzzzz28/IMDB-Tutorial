1.Introduction¶

When a new movie comes out, it always attracts people to go watch it and make comments on it. The impression of people who already watch the movie would affect others who haven’t watched it. IMDB score is one of the most reflective ratings of peoples’ impression about the movie. Not only that, the IMDB score is also related to how commercially successful the movie is. By investigating the relationship between IMDB and other factors, we could use this information to help other movie makers/investors reach their desired goal.

Now, we began wondering what factors could possibly affect the IMDB score and is it possible to predict the IMDB score based on the data we collected. In order to find this out, we use detailed data to examine the possible factors that could affect the IMDB score and determine the trend and relationship between those factors and IMDB score. We hope you would find helpful our analysis about the IMDB score and if you follow our steps, you can also learn how to use R/Python to make data analysis.

3.2 Regression and analysis¶

Relationship between IMDB score and budget¶

We want to determine the relationship between budget and IMDB score. In order to obtain a more accurate result, we need to remove some extreme values. We first standardize "budget" and then use it to remove outilers(3 standard deviation away from the mean). Then we plot the filtered data to get a "IMDB score vs budget" scatter plot.

In [10]:
stat_df = df.agg({'budget': ['mean', 'std']})
ln = len(df.index)
df['mean_bgt'] = [stat_df.budget[0]]* ln
df['std_bgt'] = [stat_df.budget[1]]* ln
df['stdzd_bgt'] = (df['budget']-df['mean_bgt'])/df['std_bgt']
temp_df = df.query('stdzd_bgt < 3 & stdzd_bgt >-3')
(ggplot(temp_df, aes(x='budget/1000000', y='imdb_score')) + geom_point()+geom_smooth(method='lm')\

Data cleaning(in R)¶

The original data is messy and contains extra information that we are not interested in investigating. The original data contains 28 columns. Data visualization (in R)¶

Before we do further data analysis about how the number of voted users would affect the IMDB score, we have a hypothesis that the higher IMDB score infers that the movie has high quality which would attract more users to see and vote for the movie. Our hypothesis is that the higher the number of voted users then the higher the IMDB score the movie would get. We plot a scatter plot of the number of voted users VS. IMDB score we also fit a trnd line using geom_smooth()

Linear regression(in R)¶

Before we try to do linear regression analysis, we test the correlation between the number of voted users and the IMDB score using Pearson correlation formula

The pearson coeficient is 0.4742 which shows that there is a weak positive linear relashionship between number of voted users and the IMDB score. Now, let’s try to create a linear regression model between the number of voted users and the IMDB score

The p-value for the intercept and the slope are both <2e-16 which is smaller than the significance level. This means this intercept of 6.125 and slope of 3.318e-06 is descriptive for this model. However the R-squared for this model is very small, which is only 0.2239. This means that only 22.39% of change in IMDB score is due to change in number of voted users. The R-squared tells us that this is not a good model to use. Based on the pearson correlation coefficient and linear regression model we can conclude that there is a weak positive linear relationship between number of voted users and the IMDB score. Now we will try to create a logistic regression model to see if it can be more descriptive about our data.

6. Conclusion and Extension¶

In this project we practiced how to use python/R to find what leads to a high IMDB score movie. according to our research, we can see that the IMDB score of a movie is influenced by multiple factors. We investigated 3 main factors, director, gross/budget and number of people voted. We found out that a good director is one of the most important factors to direct a movie with a high IMDB score. However, the gross and budget have little influence on the IMDB score. A movie with a high IMDB score would often attract more audiences to watch it and vote for it. We can see the trend that those movies that have a large number of people voted often have a higher IMDB score. Based on our research, if you are a movie lover and you are trying to find a movie you like to watch, there would be a high chance of finding a movie you prefer by looking after those movies with a high number of people who voted. You can also look after the movies directed by your favorite directors. This would help you not waste your time in looking for a movie you are interested in.
